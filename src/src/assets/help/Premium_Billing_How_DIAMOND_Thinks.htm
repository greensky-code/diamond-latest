Age Calculations and Family Roles for Premium Billing

There are several approaches to using member age as a determinant of the amount of premium charged for health care coverage. For example, some health plans bill family coverage based on the subscriber’s age. Other health plans build a family rate based on the ages of each family member. DIAMOND uses a variety of set up options and rules to define how each of these, and other, age calculations is handled and associated with the correct premium rate.

It is also important for premium billing calculations to identify who is the subscriber, who is the spouse or domestic partner, and who are non-spouse/non-domestic partner dependents.

Following are discussions of the variables that determine how ages are calculated and roles determined.

Age Calculations and Premium Billing

Some health plans associate different rates depending on the age of, most typically, the subscriber, or less common, the individual member.

DIAMOND’s Matrix Billing allows you to set up age-adjusted rates for either Member-by-Member (Whole Family) or Subscriber methods. Member-by-Member builds a family rate by adding a rate for each family member (see Member-by-Member (Whole Family) Family Billing section for specifics). Subscriber matrix billing finds a rate that applies to the entire family depending on the characteristics of that subscriber.

You can control some aspects of how DIAMOND calculates ages. Depending on the entries in GRUPB, you may calculate ages at the start of each billing period (defined according to the billing Frequency) or you may calculate ages (and rates) at the Billing Start Date and freeze those ages (and rates) for a year.

If you set a Common Billing Date for the group, DIAMOND uses that date as the Billing Start Date for all members in the group as of that date. For members added to the group after the Billing Start Date DIAMOND calculates each member’s age as of the member’s Effective Date (on MELIG) until the anniversary of the Billing State Date, at which time all current member ages are calculated as of that date for the coming year. If a member has several eligibility periods DIAMOND uses the oldest effective date wherein the member is associated with the group.

If you do not set a Common Billing Date for the group, DIAMOND uses each member’s Billing Begin Date (on MEMBB).

Whose Age?

If you are using tiering method Whole Family you can establish rates associated with the characteristics of each family member. The characteristics you can use in addition to age are gender, Medicare status, and salary.

If you are using tiering method Subscriber you establish rates associated with the characteristics of the subscriber only. The characteristics you can use in addition to age are gender, Medicare status, salary, presence or absence of a spouse or domestic partner, and family size.

Subscriber Unit Roles and Premium Billing

How DIAMOND recognizes the subscriber

For some types of premium calculations DIAMOND must recognize which family member is the subscriber. There are two different ways DIAMOND determines who the subscriber is.

  • For Member-by-Member (Whole Family) billing, the subscriber is the person with relationship code H, Head of Household.

  • For all other billing types, the subscriber is the person whose person number is the subscriber person number; technically, the member whose seq_mem_id equals the seq_sub_id.

How DIAMOND recognizes the spouse/domestic partner or non-spouse/non-domestic partner dependent

The Relationship Code of "S" identifies which member of the family is the spouse. The Relationship Code of "E" identifies which member of the family is the domestic partner. The spouse or domestic partner identity is required for tiering methods 4-step and 5-step, where different rates are applied to families with and without spouse/domestic partner and non-spouse/non-domestic partner dependents, and for Member-by-Member (Whole Family) billing, where the definition of dependent is used for certain calculations.

A non-spouse/non-domestic partner dependent is any member of the family who is not the subscriber, as defined above, and is not the spouse or domestic partner, as defined above.

DIAMOND must know a dependent, as distinguished from a spouse or domestic partner, for tiering methods 4-step and 5-step, and for Member-by-Member (Whole Family) billing.

Annual Premium Billing

Annual premium billing is a method by which a group is billed for coverage at the beginning of the plan year for the entire upcoming year.

Annual and Supplemental Bills

The first bill of the year for an annually billed group is run in GPBIL with a Bill Run Type of Regular. In each of the other months of the plan year, the Bill Run Type is Supplemental. The Supplemental bill includes charges for subscribers who are added to DIAMOND later in the year, using the annual premium amount from GRUPD for the subscriber effective date.

Note that unlike other types of billing, no adjustments are made for terminating members.

The initial annual bill includes all subscribers known to be in the group even if their effective dates are after the start of the plan year. For example, an annual bill is run in January. There are five subscribers who are enrolled, four of whom are effective January 1 and one of which is effective March 1. For a group with flat rate annual billing, each of the five subscribers is billed on the initial annual bill at the same rate. For a group with variable rate annual billing, four of the subscribers are billed at the rate from GRUPD effective January 1, and one subscriber is billed at the rate from GRUPD effective March 1. Note that in both cases, however, all five subscribers are billed on the initial annual bill.

Supplemental bills pick up those subscribers who are added to DIAMOND after the initial annual bill is run. Like any premium billing, the rate selected for those subscribers is the rate in GRUPD that applies to the period in which the subscribers become effective, not the rate that applies to the date the subscribers are added to DIAMOND.

Defining an Annually-billed Group

On the group’s GROUP record the Group Type must be Annual. This entry is system-displayed on the GRUPB record as Bill Type. On the GRUPB record the field Frequency displays Annual (based on Group Type being Annual) and no entry in this field is permitted.

Premium Tiering and Amounts

In order to bill annually, the only tiering method you may use is method 1, Subscriber only. This tiering method is selected on the group’s GRUPB record.

Unlike other billing methods, for which the premium amount entered on GRUPB is a monthly amount, for annual billing you enter the annual premium. Because only tiering method 1 is available, this is a single rate that applies to all subscribers.

Some annually billed groups charge a lower rate for subscribers who enroll later in the year so that subscribers who join later in the year are charged less than those who are eligible for the entire 12 months. In this case, the group has multiple GRUPD and associated GRUPB records for each date range to which each rate applies.

For example, a subscriber enrolled at the start of the year is charged at $100 for the year. A subscriber who enrolls in May is charged $75 for the remainder of that year.

Because age-based premiums are not allowed for annual billing DIAMOND defaults several fields on GRUPB, which govern, how ages are calculated. Rate Freeze Calc defaults to Billing Period Begin Date. Because Rate Freeze Calc is a system-defaulted field, you do not use the Common Billing Date option associated with Rate Freeze on GRUPB or the Across the Board option (which only pertains to groups using Billing Start Date to calculate member ages and rates).

Dependents

Annual billing does not make allowances for family size or other characteristics related to dependents. Only tiering method 1 is available, a single premium step which applies regardless of spouse/domestic partner or number of dependents, and options for handling dependents are irrelevant to this billing type. Thus on GRUPB both the Adult Dep Age and Max No Deps fields are protected from entry for these groups.

Retroactivity

No retroactivity is calculated for annual billing. A single rate is selected (based on the date the member becomes eligible) and applied to the entire year. Members added during the year are billed at the single rate that applies to the month (or other period) during which they became eligible. No adjustments are made for early terminations, that is, terminations within the annual period. Therefore, the field Retro Months on GRUPB is protected from entry if the Group Type is Annual.

Proration

No proration is available for annual billing, because by definition a single amount is billed for the entire year, and mid-month beginning and ending dates of eligibility are irrelevant. Therefore, the field Proration Method on GRUPB is protected from entry if the Group Type is Annual.

Administrative Fees

Annually billed groups may have administrative fees, and the associated fields in keyword GRUPB may be used.

Changing from Annual to Other Group Types

Annual billing does not employ any retroactivity. Likewise, when an annually billed group is changed to another group type, that new group type incarnation cannot capture any retroactivity from the period when the group was annually billed. Therefore, prior to changing the group type you must reconcile the annually billed period, terminate the group, and create the group anew with GROUP, GRUPD, and GRUPB records.

Billed Through and Paid Through Dates

These fields on GRUPB are system-displayed based on the most recent premium billing run and cash receipts. These fields are displayed by DIAMOND and can be overridden manually using Special menu commands in GRUPB and MEMBB.

Paid Through Date (GRUPB - Paid Through or MEMBB - Paid Through)

Each time a customer is billed for premiums (GPBIL), DIAMOND creates a historic billing record with the following information:

1) Balance Forward (prior billing period’s new balance), plus

2) Payments received and posted during the last premium billing period, plus

3) Manually entered Accounts Receivable Adjustments (ARADJ) - (retroactivity adjustments are contained in New Charges below), plus

4) New Charges (total premium charges for the billing period, retroactive premium adjustments, administration fees), equals

5) New Balance

Each time cash receipts are posted (ARCSH), DIAMOND performs a calculation(s) to determine if the customer’s Paid Through date should be updated. To perform this calculation DIAMOND uses:

  • The customer’s prior historic billing records,

  • The threshold percentage (GROUP > PTD Threshold Percent),

  • The Total Outstanding Posted Cash (all ARCSH cash receipts for the customer which have been entered and posted since the last premium billing run).

A customer is considered paid up through the current billing period when the customer’s new balance (amount owed) is less than the calculated paid through threshold amount for the billing period.

If the customer is not paid up through the current billing period, DIAMOND evaluates if, considering the Total Outstanding Post Cash, the threshold has been reached for the prior billing period. DIAMOND continues to evaluate backwards, a previous billing period at a time, until either the Paid Through Date threshold or the existing Paid Through Date is reached.

Member-by-Member (Whole Family) Family Billing

If you have a premium rate structure that builds a family rate by adding together rates for each individual family member, you may use Member-by-Member Family Billing. This kind of billing in DIAMOND allows you to select an individual member’s rate according to member age, gender, salary, Medicare status, and rate type. In addition, you may define a maximum number of dependents and maximum dependent age, which work together to define whether and how a dependent’s rate is selected from the rate matrix.

Member-by-member family billing, called Whole Family in DIAMOND because it is triggered by the Whole Family tiering method, is rarely used in the U.S. However, you may find it is a useful method for certain premium structures.

Member-by-member family billing is one of two matrix billing approaches. The other matrix billing calculation, more commonly used, selects rates for an entire family based on the characteristics of the subscriber, such as the subscriber’s age and gender and the total family size.

Defining a Member-by-Member "Whole Family" Group

On the GRUPB record the Tiering Method is Whole Family, and a Matrix Definition is entered which points to the rates in keyword PREMM (A/R Matrix Premium Rate Entry). Like other billing methods except Annual, the premium amounts themselves are monthly amounts. The Frequency on GRUPB will define how often the group is actually billed; monthly, quarterly, etc.

The matrix, where the combinations of member characteristics that define each rate cell are defined, is set up in MATRX.

Who is the Subscriber?

Unlike other premium methods, for Member-by-Member ‘Whole Family" billing DIAMOND assumes that the person designated as Head of Household (MELIG, Relationship Code) is the subscriber. This designation is important only to eliminate the subscriber from the count of the number of dependents.

Who is the Spouse or Domestic Partner and Which Dependents are Billed

Like other billing types, the spouse is defined as the member whose Relationship Code on the MELIG record is S, for Spouse. Also, the domestic partner is the member whose Relationship Code on the MELIG record is E, for Domestic Partner. This designation is important for Member-by-Member billing only to identify dependents.

Dependents are all the members of the family who are neither the Spouse nor Domestic Partner nor the Head of Household (assumed to be the subscriber).

Although this billing method is building a rate by adding together the rates for each member of the family, you may limit the number of dependents whose rates are included by setting a maximum number of dependents (Max No Deps on GRUPB). DIAMOND will, in person number order, include only as many dependents as you indicate in this field.

For example, if the Max No Deps is 3, the rate for a family of subscriber, spouse, and 5 children will comprise the rates for the subscriber (Rel Code "H"), spouse (Rel Code "S"), and three dependents, the lowest dependent person number (Per No on MEMBR) 03, the next-to-lowest dependent person number 04, and the second-to-lowest person number 05. The rates for the remaining 2 dependents are not added to the family premium.

To further refine the family’s total rate calculation you may also define a maximum age (Max Dep Age on GRUPB) so that only dependents whose age equals or is less than that age are included in the Max No Deps count.

In the example above you set the Max Dep Age to 16. Person number 05 is 17 years old, so this person number is not included within the Max No Deps, and therefore person number 06, age 11, is included in her place. Because person number 05 is over the Max Dep Age, her premium is calculated in addition to the subscriber, spouse, and three dependents. Thus, in this example, a total of 6 family members are included in the total family rate.

If the Max No Deps is blank (the field does not accept 0), the family’s rate will include only the member rates for the subscriber, the spouse or domestic partner, and any dependents whose ages exceed Max Dep Age.

Retroactive Adjustments

The following is an example of how DIAMOND calculates retroactive premium bill adjustments for retroactive changes to member eligibility dates. In this example, premium billing is being run monthly.

John Jones and dependent #1 (associated with a GROUP > Group Type = Individual and GRUPB > Retro Months = 3) are enrolled as members in September 1998 with an effective date of 4/1/98. When premium billing is run for September 1998, DIAMOND calculates an initial premium bill for John Jones and dependent #1 for the months of April through August (retroactive adjustment) and September. The GRUPB > Retro Months (3) is not used during initial customer billing retroactive calculations.

During October 1998, dependent #2 is enrolled under subscriber John Jones with an effective date of 5/1/98. When premium billing is run for October 1998, DIAMOND calculates a premium bill for:

  • Dependent #2 for the months of July through September (retroactive calculation is now based on GRUPB > Retro Months = 3). DIAMOND does not include May through June in the retroactive calculation.

  • John Jones, dependent #1 and dependent #2 for the month of October.

During March 1999, John Jones and dependents #1 and #2 are terminated with an effective date of 11/1/98. When premium billing is run for March 1999, DIAMOND calculates a premium billing credit adjustment for all three members for the months of December 1998 – February 1999 (retroactive calculation based on GRUPB > Retro Months = 3). DIAMOND does not include November 1998 in the retroactive calculation. No premium bill calculation is performed for March 1999.

See Group Master (GROUP) documentation for a complete description of Group Types.

DIAMOND software also calculates retroactive premium billing adjustments for changes to the member's plan or riders. Retroactive changes to the plan or riders on a member's eligibility record automatically trigger retroactive adjustments in the next premium billing run for the group.

Plan or rider retroactive adjustments are calculated for the full number of months specified in the GRUPB field Retro Months. This time period includes the current eligibility record and any prior eligibility records that fall within the number of Retro Months specified.

Retroactive Rate Adjustments

Premium Billing group rate retroactivity allows you to make retroactive changes that affect premium billing rates. These can be direct changes to the rates or methods themselves or changes to GRUPD Effective Dates, which cause different rates to apply at different times. When you run premium billing and group rate retroactivity applies, DIAMOND debits the old rate and credits the new rate for the applicable period for members in the group.

Premium Billing group rate retroactivity is triggered by:

  • A change to the GRUPD Effective Date of the current open period (automatic retro)*

  • A change to a PREMM rate matrix fields Matrix Calc Method and Premium Rate Entry, where all groups sharing this rate matrix are included (optional retro)

  • A change to GRUPB fields Tiering Mthd, Rate 1-5, Matrix Def, or Pct of Matrix (optional retro)

* Must be accompanied by a change to the GRUPD End Date of the prior period so that there is no gap between periods

The above GRUPD changes automatically trigger group rate retroactivity in the next premium billing run for the group. DIAMOND gives you a warning when you make a GRUPD change that will automatically trigger group rate retroactivity.

Warning:  

With rate retroactivity, you can change a rate without terming the GRUPD record, as noted above. However, if you do so, the prior rate is not stored. Thus if you terminate a member retroactively, the prior rate is not available for DIAMOND’s member level retroactivity. DIAMOND instead uses the new rate. In this case, you must use ARADJ to adjust premium billing for retroactively terminated members, adjusting for the difference between the new rate and the prior rate.

The above PREMM or GRUPB changes trigger a dialogue box that offers you the option to perform group rate retroactivity. You can:

  • Save the record with changes and perform retroactivity in the next GPBIL run for the group.

  • Save the record with changes and do not perform retroactivity. Apply the new rate for the group going forward only.

  • Do not save the change.

Warning:  

Any changes to a GRUPD record, including changes to fields triggering group rate retroactivity, could impact claims and other DIAMOND functionality. This is the case for any change which impacts eligibility information.

The following guidelines also apply to group rate retroactivity:

  • Group rate retroactivity applies to all Group Types

  • At least one billing cycle must have been run and posted for the group before DIAMOND will perform group rate retroactivity.

  • DIAMOND only performs group rate retroactivity when there is no gap between the End Date of the prior GRUPD record and the Effective Date of the current GRUPD record.

  • DIAMOND only performs group rate retroactivity for GRUPD periods where the GRUPD End Date is greater than the group’s GRUPB Billed Through date.

  • If you make more than one change to any trigger field prior to running and posting the next premium billing run, DIAMOND uses only the most recent change for group rate retroactivity.

  • Member level retroactive months (GRUPB Retro Months field) do not impact group rate retroactivity.

  • DIAMOND writes all changes triggering group rate retroactivity to an archive table for reporting purposes.

  • If you make changes that trigger both group rate retroactivity and member eligibility retroactivity, DIAMOND first applies group rate retroactivity and then member eligibility retroactivity.

Note:  

Group rate retroactivity does not apply to currently termed members. If a member is termed at the time GPBIL is run, DIAMOND does not select the member for premium billing. Thus group rate retroactivity cannot be triggered for a termed member. You must instead adjust premium billing rates for termed members by entering adjustments using keyword ARADJ (Accounts Receivable Adjustments).

Also, you cannot trigger premium billing rate retroactivity by terming a GRUPD record. Premium billing rate retroactivity is not triggered by a change only to a GRUPD End Date. To reflect a retroactively discontinued coverage, you must instead trigger premium billing member eligibility retroactivity for each affected member. First terminate each affected member's MELIG record on the date coverage was discontinued, and then enter a new MELIG record without the discontinued coverage. This triggers premium billing member eligibility retroactivity.

For example, you may want to discontinue a rider coverage retroactively. To trigger premium billing member eligibility retroactivity for this change, first terminate each affected member's corresponding MELIG record on the date coverage was discontinued. Then enter a new MELIG record for each member effective the following day without the discontinued rider. Do not term the rider GRUPD record until after the premium billing run picks up the retroactive change.

Premium Billing Output

There is no standard premium bill format resulting from the execution of GPBIL. In lieu of a standard premium bill format and data set, DIAMOND writes premium billing records to the following tables depending on which GPBIL action (GPBIL>Action>Calculate; GPBIL>Action>Post) is selected.
