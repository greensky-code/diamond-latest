Multiple Coverage Overview

Multiple Coverage functionality in DIAMOND

Multiple Coverage means that an individual has health insurance benefits from more than one carrier. Multiple Coverage functionality in DIAMOND consists of two components:

1. Dual Coverage

Occasionally an individual has membership in more than one Health Plan. This occurs when:

  • One spouse is a subscriber through his or her employer group and at the same time is enrolled as a dependent on his or her spouse’s account with another employer group

  • A member is working two jobs and he or she is the subscriber in both instances

In DIAMOND, each instance of an individual with Health Plan coverage equals one Member Master record. Since one individual can have more than one Health Plan coverage, one individual can have more than one Member Master record in DIAMOND.

A DIAMOND ID is a unique alphanumeric identifier for each person in DIAMOND. A person who has membership in more than one Health Plan can have one DIAMOND ID and multiple Member Master records. A person who has membership in one Health Plan and a single Member Master Record can also have a DIAMOND ID.

You enable DIAMOND ID in the parameter DIDSEARCH. DIAMOND ID is an optional field.

DIAMOND IDs are assigned via the MEMBR window (see MEMBR reference documentation for more information). Once they are assigned to each member in the system:

  • New subscribers can be added either by DIAMOND ID or Subscriber ID

  • A new subscriber can be added to an existing DIAMOND ID by selecting Special > Add New Subscriber to DID in the MEMBR window

  • A dependent can be added by selecting Special > Add Dependent in the MEMBR window

  • IDs can be changed by selecting Special > Modify DID in the MEMBR window.

  • Members with the same DIAMOND ID can be viewed by selecting Special > View DID Members in the MEMBR window

  • ID Cards can be printed by setting Parameter 2 of IDCARDORDER to ‘D’. See PARAM reference documentation for more information

See Dual Coverage, How DIAMOND Thinks for information about the processing logic behind DIAMOND IDs.

See Setting up DIAMOND IDs for implementation information.

 

2. Coordination of Benefits (COB)

Coordination of Benefits (COB) is the process by which medical insurance benefits of multiple coverages are coordinated for a single claim. DIAMOND COB functionality allows you to coordinate additional coverage with the coverage on the DIAMOND claim to adjust the net amount paid by DIAMOND on a particular claim. The additional coverage could be another DIAMOND coverage or it could be from a carrier external to DIAMOND.

Several different COB calculation methods are available for commercial carriers and Medicare. You can also hold or deny claims based on COB criteria and track COB history. In addition, you can define rules to determine the COB application of benefits order (Primary, Secondary, Tertiary or Other) for each individual COB History record.

See Coordination of Benefits Overview for more information.
