Manually Pricing a Claim Detail Line

Restrictions

Instructions

Frequently Asked Questions

Pricing is the determination of the allowed amount on a claim detail line. Usually, a claim detail line is automatically priced based on a particular pricing method. However, you can also manually price a claim line.

A claim detail line is automatically priced when you enter the Billed Amount and press TAB. Automatic pricing can be based on one of several methods, including fee schedules, percentage of billed charges, conversion factors, per-diems, diagnostic-related groups, or a combination of methods. The group contract, provider contract, specific procedure code, or a combination of these factors can drive the method of automatic pricing.

You can manually price a claim detail line by overriding the calculated Allowed Amount for either a new claim detail line or any existing unfinalized detail line, as described below.

Restrictions

You can only manually price an unfinalized claim detail line, that is, the Proc Status list is not set to F-Final (posted).

If you manually price a claim detail line and subsequently use the Pricing and Adjudication command on the Special menu to reprice, then DIAMOND will overwrite the Allowed Amount on any unfinalized lines.

Instructions

Step

Comment

1. Enter the claim header (or open the desired existing claim).

 

2. Enter the claim detail line through Billed Amount. DIAMOND automatically prices the line and displays the calculated Allowed Amount. (For an existing claim, select the unfinalized detail line to be changed.)

 

3. In the Allowed Amt box, type over the calculated amount and press TAB.

When you TAB out of the Allowed Amt box, DIAMOND sets the first character of the Adjudication Method field to M to indicate that the line was manually priced. DIAMOND also uses the entered Allowed Amount to perform adjudication if adjudication is required
