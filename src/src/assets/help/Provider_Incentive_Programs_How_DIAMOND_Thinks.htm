Provider Incentive Programs, How DIAMOND Thinks

A provider incentive program is an effort by an HMO to reward providers for enacting quality programs or to create a financial incentive to enact additional quality programs. For example, providers sometimes enact quality programs, such as extended hours, to increase customer satisfaction.

Payments are also offered to capitated providers who meet the user’s configurable definition of an open panel, defined as Access Program. The provider’s eligibility for the various quality programs is recorded on the provider contract record and is maintained by the user. Documentation in support of this eligibility is maintained by the user outside of the DIAMOND program. The eligibility for an Access Program payment is determined by evaluating several existing check boxes and values relating to provider enrollment. The user may also designate the provider’s eligibility for Access Program as an override of the system evaluation. A new keyword holds information on which Quality Programs and Access Program are in effect, provider selection criteria, and the amount per enrolled member or percent of capitation to be applied. The amount is multiplied by the provider’s enrolled members during calculation of capitation, and the result is included in the capitation check. The user can also specify the amount of the payment as a percentage of the capitation payment.

Calculating capitation payments

When calculating the Incentive Program payment, capitation calculation will look at the rules in keyword INRUL (Incentive Rules) to determine which programs are in effect for a provider. The system will then look at the provider’s contract record in keyword PROVC (Provider Contract) and the provider enrollment record in keyword PROVE (Provider Enrollment) to determine if the provider is eligible for the particular program. If so, the dollar amount on INRUL and the provider’s enrolled members are used to determine the total payment, which is added to the capitation payment. If the provider is eligible for more than one payment, the amounts are additive.
