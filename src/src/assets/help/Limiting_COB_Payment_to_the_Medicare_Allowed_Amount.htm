Limiting COB Payment to the Medicare Allowed Amount

Restrictions

Instructions

Frequently Asked Questions

You can apply a COB calculation method for Medicare other coverage when adjudicating professional services and institutional claims. You can also, on each provider contract, choose to limit the total claim payment to at most the Medicare Allowed Amount. This means that you can limit the DIAMOND Net Amount paid such that the sum of the DIAMOND Net Amount plus the Medicare payment does not exceed the Medicare allowed amount.

Restrictions

To limit COB payment to the Medicare Allowed Amount, you must enter the Medicare Allowed Amount on the appropriate claim detail lines in the field OC Allowed amount. DIAMOND does not calculate the Medicare Allowed amount.

Instructions

Step

Comment

1. Determine the employee group and provider contracts for which you want to limit payment to the Medicare Allowed Amount

 

2. On the selected provider contracts (PROVC), select the PROVC MCare Assign check box.

 

3. Set up COB in the normal fashion, including selecting the appropriate COB calculation method for the group detail records (GRUPD) from among the following:

  • 7 - COB to Coordination

  • 8 - COB to Supplemental

  • 9 - COB: Benefit Carve Out

  • 10 - Medicare the Lesser Of

You can only apply this limit when you use one of the four COB calculation methods noted.

You must enter the Medicare Allowed Amount on the appropriate claim detail lines in the field OC Allowed amount. DIAMOND does not calculate the Medicare Allowed amount.

When a claim is priced using covering provider’s logic and then adjudicated using a COB calculation method that uses the PROVC Medicare Assignment check box value, DIAMOND uses the Medicare Assignment check box value from the same provider contract as was used to price the claim. For covering providers, the contract used to price the claim is specified on the corresponding Covering Provider Groups (CVPVG) record. On the CVPVG record, the Reimbursement Method field specifies whether to use the parent provider’s (the provider being covered) contract or the covering provider’s contract.

4. Process claims for the group.
