Administrative Fee Reason Codes, How DIAMOND Thinks

You can add a reason code to store the reason why a provider was charged an administrative fee by a health plan, or why a health plan was charged an administrative fee by a provider. Then, when you add an administrative fee rule using keyword ADRUL (Provider Administrative Fee Rule), you can select an administrative fee reason code to indicate the reason why an administrative fee was charged.

Administrative fees are sometimes referred to as service administrative fees. They can be fees charged to a provider by a health plan for not completing some sort of task in the required manner. For example, if the provider does not provide sufficient information on a submitted claim.

Administrative fees can also be fees paid to a provider by a health plan for completing a task in the desired manner. For example, a provider may receive a bonus from a health plan for sending claims electronically instead of on paper.
