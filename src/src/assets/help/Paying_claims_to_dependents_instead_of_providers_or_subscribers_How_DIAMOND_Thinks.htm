Paying claims to dependents instead of providers or subscribers, How DIAMOND Thinks

You can pay a claim to the dependent instead of the provider or the subscriber. This may be convenient if the dependent does not want to alert the subscriber to an institutional, professional services, or dental claim. DIAMOND uses the address of the dependent from the Subscriber ID/Person No. record in keyword MEMBR (Member Master). In addition, you can change the address of a dependent without affecting the subscriber’s address.

When you select to pay a dependent, the Pay Dep box will be set to Y on the claim header window. When you save the claim, DIAMOND does the following:

  • Prices the claim according to the original provider selected on the claim.

  • Adds a vendor record in keyword VENDR (Vendor Master) with the dependent’s name from keyword MEMBR. The Vendor ID is prefixed with "SD." Any future payments to this dependent will be paid based on the information for this vendor record.

  • Adds a vendor address record in keyword VENDA (Vendor Address) with the dependent’s address from keyword MEMBR. If you later change the dependent’s address in keyword MEMBR, then the dependent’s vendor address record in keyword VENDA will automatically change.

DIAMOND uses the dependent’s name and address when adding the A/P record in keyword ACPAY (Accounts Payable) and payment check in keyword CKPRT (Check Print Setup). DIAMOND changes the RA/EOB Print Flag list to 1 – Paper EOB and No RA on the A/P record in keyword ACPAY.

If you change information on the claim by using the Change Header command on the Special menu, then the Pay Dep box will be set to N. To pay the claim to the dependent, again you must select the Pay Dependent command on the Special menu.

For more information, see Paying a claim to the dependent of a subscriber.
