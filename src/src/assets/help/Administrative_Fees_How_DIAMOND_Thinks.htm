Administrative Fees, How DIAMOND Thinks

Health plans sometimes offer administrative fees to providers who submit complete and appropriate data on qualifying services they provide to their patients.

Administrative fees are sometimes referred to as service administrative fees. They can be fees charged to a provider by a health plan for not completing some sort of task in the required manner. For example, if the provider does not provide sufficient information on a submitted claim.

Administrative fees can also be fees paid to a provider by a health plan for completing a task in a desired manner. For example, a provider may receive a bonus from a health plan for sending claims electronically instead of on paper.

You can add administrative fee rules using keyword ADRUL (Provider Administrative Fee Rule) to define the services that are considered when determining an administrative fee. You can include selection criteria, such as procedure code, primary diagnosis code, age of member, and whether the submitted claim is clean or unclean.

For more information about adding administrative fee rules, see Setting Up Administrative Fees.

When a service meets the rule determinants and other specified criteria, the administrative fee is charged. The transaction is then finalized, an accounts payable record is created, and the vendor is paid during the normal accounts payable process in a separate check. Claims are evaluated for administrative fees and transaction records are created in a batch process in keyword JOBAD (Administrative Fee Batch Job).

If a claim is reversed or adjusted after calculating administrative fees, the administrative fees are not reversed automatically. You must manually reverse administrative fees.

You can finalize only administrative fee transactions, or you can finalize administrative fee transactions along with other claims and advance pay transactions.

You can use keyword CKPRT to generate just an administrative fee check or you can use CKPRT to print checks for administrative fees, advance payment, and claims. In any of these cases, a separate check is generated for administrative fees and advance payment.
