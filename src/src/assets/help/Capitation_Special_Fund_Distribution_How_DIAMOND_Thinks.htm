DIAMOND allows you to pay providers an allocation from a specified fund of dollars. These funds may be established as part of a capitation arrangement, but otherwise are not associated with traditional capitation. Paying providers from a specified fund is referred to in DIAMOND as Capitation/Special Fund Distribution (CFD), or Cap/Special Fund for short.

Relationship: Pricing and Cap/Special Fund Distribution

When you price an INCLM/PSCLM claim detail line, DIAMOND performs the provider pricing contract search. If the provider contract found includes a Contract Type of Capitation Funding and a Cap/Special Fund (CFD) Model, DIAMOND assigns a Claim Status of F (Cap Fund) and a Cap Fund Status of 1 (Cap Fund) to the claim line. A Cap Fund Status of 1 indicates that the claim line should be evaluated for inclusion in any Cap/Special Fund batch runs (CFDST) for the corresponding Model. Pricing also adds the Cap Fund Model ID and Cap Fund Submodel ID from the provider contract to the claim detail line record, as well as total unit value if the line was priced using the Unit Value pricing calculation method. You can view this Cap Fund information using the INCLM/PSCLM Special menu command View Cap Fund Info.

If the provider contract found does not have a Contract Type of Cap Fund, DIAMOND assigns a Cap Fund Status of 0 (Not a Cap Fund) and the claim line will not be included in any CFDST runs.

Cap/Special Fund Distribution

Note— Generally, you will want to wait several weeks after the end of a Cap/Special Fund Distribution month before running the distribution for that month. This allows all claim lines (or nearly all claim lines) with service dates in the capitation month to be entered, priced and adjudicated, and thus to be ready for Cap/Special Fund Distribution processing.

When you run a CFDST batch job in Run mode (edit mode), DIAMOND performs the following processing logic:

1. Selects Qualifying Claim Detail Lines

For the CFD Model ID, DIAMOND selects "qualifying" claim detail lines (PSCLM and/or INCLM) for the capitation fund distribution process. Claim lines qualify if they meet the following requirements:

  • Claim line has been priced, or priced and adjudicated as noted on the corresponding GRUPD record (Group Req)

  • Provider Contract found for the Provider of service and date of service on the claim includes a Contract Type of Capitation Funding and the Capitation Fund Model ID of the model being processed

  • Capitation Fund Process Status is 1 (Cap Fund).

  • Processing Status is not Hold (Header holds which do not set the detail line Process Status to Hold do not affect Capitation Fund Distribution.)

  • Claim Status is not Denied.

  • From Date of service falls within the date range specified on the CFDST job or the claim line has been manually flagged as a Capitation Fund payable, that is, flagged to qualify regardless of the From Date specified on the CFDST job. (You flag detail lines using the PSCLM/INCLM Special menu command – Override Cap Fund Dates.) More specifically, unless flagged for Cap/Special Fund (CF) Distribution the claim detail line from Date must fall between the CFDST From date and the last day of the CFD Distribution month being run in order to qualify. (The specified CFDST From date must be equal to or less than the first day of the month for the CFDST Cap Month being run).

  • Claim detail line Audit Status is not set to Y (Yes).

  • All other claim lines on the same claim satisfy all requirements to qualify.

Important:

Again, in order for any claim detail line to be selected for Cap/Special Fund processing, all lines on the claim must qualify. All claim detail lines on a claim must be included in a single CFDST run. If a claim has multiple detail lines, all lines must meet all requirements for qualifying or none of the lines will be selected by the CFDST run.

So for example, if a claim includes 20 claim detail lines and one has a Process Status of Hold, DIAMOND will not select any lines on the claim for Cap/Special Fund Distribution regardless of whether the other 19 lines qualify. The hold must be relieved for any lines on this claim to be included.

Also, if a claim includes detail lines with From Dates of service in two or more months, you should split the claim by month in order to include the claim in Cap/Special Fund Distribution processing. Otherwise, if you run a monthly CFDST run with a date range specific to each month, DIAMOND will not select lines from this claim. See the examples below.

Date Requirement, Example 1

The claim includes the following two detail lines, with From Dates which fall in a single month:

2. Sums All Qualifying Claim Lines

After DIAMOND selects all qualifying claim lines, DIAMOND totals either the Allowed Amounts or Net Amounts (based on the CFMOD Allocation Method you specified for the current Model) for all the qualifying claim lines for all providers.

3. Calculates CFD Distribution Percentage

DIAMOND calculates a CF Distribution Allocation Rate to use as a basis for calculating an Adjusted Net Amount for each of the qualifying claim detail lines.

DIAMOND calculates the CF Distribution Allocation Rate as:

CFD Distribution Allocation Rate=

CFDST Allocation amount ÷Total Allowed Amounts or Net Amounts for all qualifying claim lines (based on the CFMOD – Allocation Method )

If the CFDST Allocation amount is zero, DIAMOND uses the CFMOD Minimum Allocation percentage for the current Model as the CF Distribution Allocation Rate. The CFDST Allocation amount cannot be negative.

4. Compares Distribution Allocation Rate to the CFMOD Min/Max Percentages

Before calculating the Adjusted Net Amounts on the Claim detail lines, DIAMOND compares the CF Distribution Allocation Rate to the CFMOD Minimum Allocation and Maximum Allocation percentage values you specified for the current model.

DIAMOND selects the CFD Allocation Rate to use as follows:

If the CFD Distribution Allocation Rate is greater than (or equal to) the CFMOD Min Allocation % and less than (or equal to) the CFMOD Max Allocation %

Then use CF Distribution Allocation Rate

If the CFD Distribution Allocation Rate is less than the CFMOD Min Allocation %

Then use CFMOD Min Allocation % as the new CF Distribution Allocation Rate

If the CFD Distribution Allocation Rate is more than the CFMOD Max Allocation %

Then use CFMOD Max Allocation % as the new CF Distribution Allocation Rate

5. Calculates Adjusted Net Amounts and Cap Fund Withhold Amounts

DIAMOND calculates Adjusted Net Amounts and Cap Fund Withhold Amounts for each qualifying claim detail line as follows (using the CF Distribution Allocation Rate from Step 4):

If the CFMOD Allocation Method for the current model is Net Amount:

Then Adjusted Net Amount = Original Net Amount x CF Distribution Allocation Rate

And Cap Fund Withhold Amount = Adjusted Net Amount - Original Net Amount

If the CFMOD Allocation Method for the current model is Allowed Amount:

Then Adjusted Net Amount = Original Allowed Amount x Distribution Allocation Rate

And Cap Fund Withhold Amount = Adjusted Net Amount - Original Net Amount

Note-

It is possible for DIAMOND to calculate an Adjusted Net Amount greater than 100% of the original Net Amount. This can occur when the Total Allowed Amounts or Net Amounts (per the CFMOD Allocation Method) for all qualifying claim lines is less than the CFDST Allocation for the month and the CFMOD Maximum Allowance % for the model is greater than 100%.

6. Updates Status When CFDST Run (edit) Mode Job Completes Successfully

When the CFDST Run mode job is completed successfully, the claim line is updated with a Cap Fund Status of 2 (Successful Run). (You can view CFD information using the INCLM/PSCLM Special menu command View Cap Fund Info.) The CFDST job Status is set to Successful Run. The Net Amount, Cap Fund Withhold amount and Cap Fund Run Month are not updated until you review the CFDST Summary Audit Report and Detail Audit Report, and run CFDST in Post mode. (Reviewing the audit reports is not required to run CFDST in Post mode, but is recommended. If you cancel the job, the audit reports are no longer available.)

See CFDST reference documentation for information on these audit reports.

7. Posts Results

When you run the CFDST job in Post mode, DIAMOND posts the results of the Run (edit) mode job, including the following:

  • Updates each claim detail line with the Adjusted Net Amount, Capitation Withhold Amount and Cap Fund Status of 3 (Posted) and the Cap Fund Run Month.

  • DIAMOND calculates the Capitation Fund Withhold Amount for each detail line as the original Net Amount (prior to CFD) minus the Adjusted Net Amount (after CFD). A Cap Fund Withhold Amount may be negative. Note that there are two separate withhold amount fields on the claim line, the Withhold Amount and Cap Fund Withhold Amount. Each may be assigned it’s own general ledger information and reflected on a Remittance Advice (RA), Explanation of Benefits (EOB) or Explanation of Payments (EOP).

  • Note that once the Cap Fund Status is 3 (Posted), the claim line is available for evaluation to be selected for accounts payable processing. See APUPD reference documentation for more information on accounts payable processing.

  • Updates the CFDST job Status to Posted.

  • The CFDST job results are saved in an historical table that you can access with a report writer for ad hoc reporting purposes.

8. Generates Final Cap/Special Fund Distribution Reports

Once a CFDST job is successfully completed, you may view or print audit reports for the job using CFDST Special menu commands Summary Audit Report and Detail Audit Report.

See CFDST reference documentation for information on these reports.

Claims with Various Status Codes on Different Detail Lines:

When a claim is submitted and the rendering provider only has a Cap/Special Fund contract, then all the claim lines should price using that Cap/Special Fund contract. Should that claim adjudicate normally with all the lines being eligible for payment less copays, deductibles, and coinsurance, the lines will have a Processing status of U, unposted. These lines then will be eligible for the CFD batch run for their CFD Model and month so long as the From Date of Service on the detail line falls in period from the Cap Fund From Date through the last day of the fund month being run.

Should DIAMOND deny a claim line as a not covered service (for example, a cosmetic procedure not covered in the benefit plan) the claim line will price using the Cap Fund contract for the rendering provider. Adjudication will change the Claim Status of the one denied line to Denied and change the Processing status to U, Unposted. You may then be left with at least one line with a Claim Status of Cap Fund and one line with a Claim Status of Denied. Only the Cap Fund Claim status line is eligible for the CFD batch run.

The above situation will be the same if the lines are changed to another status, for example, if they are made Informational (like in INCLM), or if they are a Payable line, or a Capitated Service.

Also, under any of these circumstances, should any of the claim lines have a Processing Status of Held, then none of the claim lines will "qualify" for the Cap Fund distribution run.

Use of the Cap Fund Override Flag:

When using the Override Cap Fund Dates flag (INCLM/PSCLM - Override Cap Fund Dates), all claim lines that meet the CFDST DOS criteria need to be flagged in order for the Cap Fund line (claim status of F) to be included in the CF run. These other claim lines on the claim must be flagged as Override Cap Fund Dates even though they do not have a Claim Status of Cap Fund. (These lines do not get picked up for the CF run, but they must be flagged in order for the Cap Fund line to get picked up by the CF run. This is due to the way that DIAMOND evaluates picking up whole claims for Cap/Special Fund processing.)

Note –

It is important when using the Cap Fund Flag Override to put the flag on all the claim lines prior to any claim line being finalized. If a claim has finalized lines on it prior to putting the override flag on all the lines, it will not be picked up for a CFD run. The only way to get claim lines (F) included in CFD processing when there are already Finalized lines on the claim is to:

  • Set the override flags when the claim is entered so that every line has the flag on it before it gets picked up in an APUPD run.

  • Alternatively, if one or more claim lines Finalize, you can include the lines in a CFD batch run by:

  • Adjusting the Cap Fund From Date, or

  • Manually splitting the claim and creating a new claim just for the CFD lines, then using the Cap Fund Override flag on all the new claim lines.
