Pricing Anesthesia Time, How DIAMOND Thinks

When a claim for anesthesia services is processed in keyword PSCLM (Professional Services Claims), you enter either the total anesthesia time in minutes or you enter the start time and end time and DIAMOND calculates the total anesthesia time, then converts the total time into anesthesia units.

This conversion from minutes to anesthesia units can be constant (10 minutes always equals one unit) or can be tiered by different numbers of minutes over the course of the anesthesia session. For example, in the first hour of anesthesia 20 minutes can equal one unit, the second hour 15 minutes equals one unit, and so forth. Up to four tiers of minutes-to-units can be set up.

In addition, this conversion of minutes-to-units can change; DIAMOND accommodates one current conversion and one prior conversion with an effective date.

1. An anesthesia time of 40 minutes (claim dated on or after 1/1/95) is converted to 2 units:

40/20 = 2 with no remainder minutes.

2. An anesthesia time of 24 minutes (claim dated on or after 1/1/95) is converted to 1 unit:

24/20 = 1 with a remainder of 4 minutes (the 4 minute remainder is less than the minimum minutes required for a whole unit; DIAMOND does not round up to an additional whole unit).

3. An anesthesia time of 26 minutes (claim dated on or after 1/1/95) is converted to 2 units:

26/20 = 1 with a remainder of 6 minutes

4. An anesthesia time of 167 minutes (claim dated on or after 1/1/95) is converted to 12 units:

60/20 = 3 - Tier One maximum reached, remainder of 107 minutes;

60/15 = 4 - Tier Two maximum reached, remainder of 47 minutes;

47/10 = 4 - Tier Three with a remainder of 7 minutes (the 7 minute remainder is greater than or equal to the minimum minutes required for a whole unit; DIAMOND rounds up to an additional whole unit); 3+ 4 + 5 = 12 units.

Anesthesia Modifier codes and Professional Modifier Pricing

If you are using Professional Modifier Pricing, then in keyword PSCLM, if you enter anesthesia Modifier codes in the first and second Mods box, then DIAMOND will use only the anesthesia Modifier code entered in the first Mods box. The Claim Detail Anesthesia Time dialog box will display twice; once for each anesthesia Modifier code. However, the second dialog box will display the values entered from the first anesthesia Modifier code. If necessary, you can manually add the values together, and then type new values in the Claim Detail Anesthesia Time dialog box.

For more information about Professional Modifier Pricing, see Modifier codes, How DIAMOND Thinks.
