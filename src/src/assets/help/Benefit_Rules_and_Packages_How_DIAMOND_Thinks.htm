Benefit rules are individual records describing the administration of a single benefit, for example, an office visit copayment, a family deductible for inpatient services, or a limit on the number of acupuncture visits in a year.

Benefit rules are set up in keyword BRULE (Benefit Rule) using a two-tier structure. In the heading or first tier of the BRULE window, general information that applies to all types of benefits is entered. In the body, or second tier of the screen a unique set of fields and options is entered, depending on the type of benefit selected (copayment, limit, etc.).

This document explains some of the common features of BRULE, examples of calculations for different benefit rule types, and general information about how benefit rules are evaluated.

Types of Benefit Rules

There are currently 8 different types of benefit rules. Within each type, different selections will result in different kinds of calculations. And, depending on the order (see keyword BORDR user documentation) in which each individual rule is evaluated during claims processing, different results are calculated.

The available types are:

  • 00: Copayments

  • 10: Coinsurance

  • 20: Limits

  • 30: Deductibles

  • 40: Out of pocket maximums

  • 50: Message and hold

  • 60: Exclusions

  • 70: (reserved for future functionality)

  • 80: Full coverage (also known as ‘first dollar coverage’)

Assembling Benefit Rules into Benefit Packages

A benefit package is a set of benefit rules applied in a particular order. Mechanically, defining the following creates a package:

  • A library of reusable benefit rules in keyword BRULE

  • A benefit rule ordering structure in keyword BORDR

  • A benefit package by associating a BORDR rule and a set of BRULE records in a single BENEF record.

Common Characteristics of Benefit Rules

Benefit rules are created once in keyword BRULE and are used in many different combinations in various benefit packages

Benefit rules are, within a benefit package, date sensitive. Over time a benefit package can be adjusted by ending benefit rules as of specific dates and adding new rules with new effective dates.

Within a benefit package a rule can be further tailored to change the way it is applied. The tailoring options are:

  • Effective date

  • End date

  • Presence of a preauthorization

  • Participation status of the rendering provider

  • Presence or absence of a rider on the member’s eligibility record

These features extend the functionality of benefit rules beyond the characteristics of the rules themselves.

How Benefit Rules are Applied to a Claim Detail Line

When a claim detail line is adjudicated, the DIAMOND system:

  • Determines the member’s Group ID/Plan Code using the member’s eligibility record (MELIG) in effect as of the claim date of service.

  • Determines the member’s benefit package (the benefit package linked to the Group ID/Plan Code) using the Group Detail record (GRUPD > Benefit Pkg) in effect as of the claim date of service.

  • Determines all of the benefit rules, associated with the benefit package, that were in effect as of the claim date of service using Benefit Package Maintenance (BENEF) record.

  • Determines which of the benefit rules apply to the claim detail line being adjudicated. If a claim detail line matches the criteria specified for multiple benefit rules that have overlapping effective dates, all the matching rules will be applied.

  • Determines the order in which the benefit rules will be applied.

  • Determines the benefit rule(s) that may be applied to the claim detail line being adjudicated based on a correlation between the medical definition specified in the benefit rule(s) (BRULE > Med Def) and the medical definition assigned to the claim detail line.

  • Applies the appropriate benefit rule(s) to the claim detail line.

DIAMOND continues to process the selected benefit rule(s) to determine if all of the rule attributes apply to the claim detail line being adjudicated. The rule may or may not be applied depending on the member’s rider codes, whether the rendering provider is a participating or non-participating provider, whether the claim has been linked to an authorization, etc.

Medical Definition Assignment

The first step DIAMOND takes to adjudicate a claim line is to calculate the medical definition for the detail line. The medical definition, or detailed type of service, is table-driven and based on user-defined determinants. In other words, you decide which characteristics of the claim are relevant to categorizing the medical service into a medical definition. Then, specific values for those characteristics are associated with different medical definition codes.

For example, an office visit may be defined by the procedure rendered and the place of service. Procedure code and place of service are the determinants. The specific procedures which represent office visits, and the place of service code which represents physician office, result in a medical definition code ‘office visit.’

See How to Change Medical Definitions and Set Up Searches, for more detailed information.

The Contract Year

Except for Message and Hold and Exclusions rules, all benefit rule types require a Time Frame be defined, the period of time over which dollar amounts and quantities accumulate to limits or maximums. DIAMOND provides five options for time frame: Calendar year (may be multiple years); Elapsed years (a number of years counting back from the detail date of service); Life; Elapsed days (counting back from the detail date of service); and Contract year.

Contract year may work in either of two ways. If the member’s eligibility record has a Benefit Start Date, contract year benefits are accumulated from that date forward for exactly one year. E.g., if the Benefit Start Date is 3/1/99, the calculated end date is 2/29/00. If there is no Benefit Start Date for the member, the contract year starts on the Effective Date and ends on the Term Date of the Group Contract (GRUPC).

Minimum and Maximum Quantities

In some cases copayments apply only when the number of services are fewer than or more than specified quantities. In other cases, the amount of copayment changes depending on the number of services already provided to the member.

For example, a benefit plan may require a $10 copayment for the first 10 physical therapy visits and a $15 copayment for any additional visits. Two copayment rules are established. The first rule specifies a $10 copayment with a minimum quantity of 0 and a maximum quantity of 10. The second copayment rule specifies a $15 copayment with a minimum quantity of 11 and a maximum quantity of 9999999999.

When DIAMOND sums up accumulated benefit quantities, if the quantity on the current claim line "straddles" the minimums or maximums, DIAMOND displays a "manual adjudication required" message and the claim is held.

Calculating Maximums

Many benefits are defined in terms of maximums, such as the maximum number of visits a year, maximum dollar amounts for certain kinds of services, maximum out of pocket for a member, and so forth.

In order to determine whether a maximum has been reached, DIAMOND must know which accumulators should be included in the count, you tell DIAMOND which accumulators to include according to a combination of medical definitions and reason codes. Only those accumulators that meet the combinations you define in BRULE are counted toward the maximum.

To specify which medical definitions and reason codes are included in the count toward the maximum, you use three menu commands:

  • Count Toward Max (for reason codes)

  • Med Def (for medical definitions)

  • Other Med Defs (for medical definitions)

The menu commands work in the same way: you tell DIAMOND either which codes (reason or med def) should be specifically evaluated, OR, if it is easier, which codes should be specifically excluded from evaluation. Which you choose depends on how many codes there are, and whether it is simpler to list the inclusions or the exclusions.

DIAMOND evaluates accumulators by a combination of the medical definition and the reason code. The medical definitions identified in Med Def and Other Med Defs which meet the reason code condition defined in Count Toward Max are included. The current claim line is also included in the calculation.

Finally, note that held or denied claim lines are not included in determining whether maximums have been reached.

When the member or family copayment or coinsurance maximum has been reached, DIAMOND calculates the claim line’s copay amount as $0.00 and leaves the copay reason field blank.

Reason Codes and Troubleshooting Adjudication

The reason code assigned to a detail line when automatically adjudicating is the key to tracing back how the calculations were done. Therefore, to the extent possible, use different reason codes for each benefit rule, this allows you to trace to a specific benefit rule.

Benefit Renewal Periods

Benefit renewal periods apply to institutional claims only and are allowed for the following benefit rule types (BRULE):

  • 00: Copayments

  • 10: Coinsurance

  • 20: Limits

  • 30: Deductibles

Definitions:

  • Limits define member/family dollar amount and/or quantity of service benefit limits. For example, there could be a maximum of $2000 for Rehab or a maximum of 45 days of psychiatric care during a calendar year.

  • Patient Liabilities include the copayment, coinsurance, and deductible amounts (out-of-pocket expenses) a patient (member) must pay when admitted to a hospital/facility.

  • A Benefit Period is the length of time a member receives benefits wherein patient liabilities are taken one time only. That is, patient liabilities are taken when the member receives a first-time or renewed benefit (initiating a benefit period) by incurring a hospital/facility admission/discharge. If, during this benefit period, the member receives the same benefit as a result of subsequent hospital/facility admissions/discharges, patient liabilities are not taken.

  • A Benefit Renewal Period is the length of time, following a benefit period that must elapse during which a member does not incur subsequent, same service claims. After this time has elapsed, DIAMOND "renews" the benefit and the member is entitled to another benefit period (initiated by a subsequent, same service claim) wherein patient liabilities will be taken.

Considerations

  • A renewal period and associated time frames must be defined (using BRULE) for each benefit rule that has a benefit period.

  • Benefit periods are measured from the first date of service (claim header - admit date) or the last date of service (claim header - discharge date).

  • Benefits periods can be renewed after a period of time (renewal period) based on:

1. Number of elapsed days

2. Number of months

3. Number of years

  • If a member incurs claims for covered services during a benefit period (patient liabilities are taken) and then does not incur any claims for the same services throughout the defined renewal period, the member’s benefit period is "renewed." If the member incurs claims for the same covered services after renewal, patient liabilities are taken again.

  • If a member incurs claims for covered services during a benefit period (patient liabilities are taken) and then incurs claims for the same services during the defined renewal period, the member’s benefit period is not "renewed." Patient liabilities are not taken for the claims incurred during the renewal period.

  • Claims that have been denied during and after the adjudication process are included when DIAMOND calculates renewal periods.

  • Claims that have been held and/or denied as a result of limit benefit rules are not included when DIAMOND calculates benefit days used.

  • DIAMOND uses the claim header admit date (INCLM, Claim Header – Adm Date) or the discharge date (INCLM, Claim Header – Disch Date) when calculating renewal periods.

  • If a claim’s total admit days are greater than the number of benefit limit days remaining, you must hold the associated claim detail line and manually split the line into a payable days line and a denied days line.

Elapsed Days

If the benefit rule renewal period (BRULE – Renewal) is based on elapsed days, the benefit is renewed on the day after the entered number of days (BRULE – Yrs./Months/Days) has passed. The following examples illustrate how DIAMOND calculates the renewal period depending on whether the calculation is based on the claim’s discharge date or admit date.

Example #1 (From Discharge Date):

BRULE – Renewal = Elapsed Days

BRULE – Yrs/Months/Days = 30

BRULE – From = Disch Date

Member’s INCLM, Claim Header – Disch Date = 9/14/99

The member’s benefit period is eligible for renewal effective 10/15/99 and forward if the member has not incurred subsequent claims for the same benefit during the renewal period (9/15/99 through and including 10/14/99).

To determine if the member is within a renewed benefit period during claim adjudication, DIAMOND counts backward (the number of days) from and including the day prior to the admit date of subsequent same service.

Using our example, if the member had a subsequent same service claim with an admit date of 10/14/99, DIAMOND:

  • Counts backward 30 days from and including 10/13 through and including 9/14 (the 30th day)

  • Searches for previous same service claims during this period

  • Finds the previous claim with the 9/14 discharge date

  • Does not take patient liabilities (benefit not renewed) for the 10/14 claim.

On the other hand, if the member had a subsequent same service claim with an admit date of 10/15/99, DIAMOND

  • Counts backward 30 days from and including 10/14 through and including 9/15 (the 30th day)

  • Searches for previous same service claims during this period

  • Does not find any previous claims during the renewal period

  • Renews the benefit

  • Takes patient liabilities for the 10/15 claim.

Example #2 (From Admit Date):

BRULE – Renewal = Elapsed Days

BRULE – Yrs/Months/Days = 30

BRULE – From = Admit Date

Member’s INCLM, Claim Header – Admit Date = 9/14/99

The member’s benefit period is eligible for renewal effective 10/14/99 and forward if the member has not incurred subsequent claims for the same benefit during the renewal period (9/15/99 through and including 10/14/99). Discharge dates are not considered in the renewal period calculation.

During claim adjudication, DIAMOND counts backward (the number of days) from and including the admit date of subsequent same service claims to determine if the member is within a renewed benefit period.

Using our example, if the member had a subsequent same service claim with an admit date of 10/13/99, DIAMOND:

  • Counts backward 30 days from and including 10/13 through and including 9/14 (the 30th day)

  • Searches for previous same service claims with an admit date during this period

  • Finds the previous claim with the 9/14 admit date

  • Does not take patient liabilities (benefit not renewed) for the 10/13 claim

On the other hand, if the member had a subsequent same service claim with an admit date of 10/14/99, DIAMOND:

  • Counts backward 30 days from and including 10/14 through and including 9/15 (the 30th day)

  • Searches for previous same service claims with an admit date during this period

  • Does not find any previous claims during the renewal period

  • Renews the benefit

  • Takes patient liabilities for the 10/14 claim
