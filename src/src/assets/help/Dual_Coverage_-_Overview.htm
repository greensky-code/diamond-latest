Dual Coverage - Overview

Background

Occasionally an individual has membership in more than one health plan. This can occur when:

  • One spouse is a subscriber through his or her employer group and at the same time is enrolled as a dependent on his or her spouse’s account with another employer group

  • A member is working two jobs and he or she is the subscriber in both instances

The use of DIAMOND IDs allows dual coverage to be tracked in DIAMOND. A person may have more than one Member Master record in keyword MEMBR (Member Master) but has only one DIAMOND ID.

An example is a woman who has coverage through her place of employment where she is the subscriber in the Member Master record (DIAMOND Subscriber Number XXXX, Person Number 01). She is also insured through her husband’s employer where she is the spouse in the Member Master record (DIAMOND Subscriber Number XXXX, Person Number 02). Both groups are in DIAMOND. The woman has one DIAMOND ID but there are two Member Master records for her in DIAMOND. The Dual Coverage Indicator on the MEMBR window shows that this situation exists.

In DIAMOND, each instance of an individual with health plan coverage equals one Member Master record. This means that one individual could have more than one Member Master record in DIAMOND.

A DIAMOND ID is a unique alphanumeric identifier for each person in DIAMOND. A person who has membership in more than one health plan or group will have one DIAMOND ID but multiple Member Master records.

DIAMOND IDs are assigned via the MEMBR window (see MEMBR reference documentation for more information). Once they are assigned to each member in the system:

  • New subscribers can be added either by DIAMOND ID or Subscriber ID

  • A new subscriber can be added to an existing DIAMOND ID by selecting Special > Add New Subscriber to DID in the MEMBR window

  • A dependent can be added by selecting Special > Add Dependent in the MEMBR window

  • IDs can be changed by selecting Special > Modify DID in the MEMBR window.

  • Members with the same DIAMOND ID can be viewed by selecting Special > View DID Members in the MEMBR window

  • ID Cards can be printed by setting Parameter 2 of IDCARDORDER to ‘D’. See PARAM reference documentation for more information

See Dual Coverage, How DIAMOND Thinks for information about the processing logic behind DIAMOND IDs.
