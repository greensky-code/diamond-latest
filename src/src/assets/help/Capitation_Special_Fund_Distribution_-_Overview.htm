Capitation/Special Fund Distribution - Overview

You can pay providers an allocation from a specified fund of dollars. These funds may be established as part of a capitation arrangement, but otherwise are not associated with traditional capitation. Paying providers from a specified fund is referred to in DIAMOND as Capitation/Special Fund Distribution (CFD), or Cap/Special Fund for short. Specifically, Cap/Special Fund allows you to distribute a specified amount of money to a group of providers based on claims utilization. All institutional and professional service claims are eligible to qualify for CFD processing, whether entered online, through claims batch, or via claims API. Certain rules apply to determine which of the eligible claims actually qualify.

An overview of the Cap/Special Fund process is presented in the following diagram.

Cap/Special Fund Distribution Process Overview

Using Cap/Special Fund Distribution, you can:

  • Control the total fund dollar amount distributed to group of providers

  • Prorate the portion of this total fund amount paid to each individual provider based on claims activity, using either claim detail line Allowed Amount or Net Amount.

For example, you can use Cap/Special Fund Distribution to pay a group of specialist providers from a special fund. Specialists, unlike primary care physicians, typically do not have assigned members and payment on a strict per member per month basis is not possible. CFD gives you a way to control the total dollar amount distributed to such a group. You can set up models to specify whether the prorated percentage of the fund amount to be paid on each claim line is based on total Allowed Amounts or total Net Amounts.

Based on this proration logic, Cap/Special Fund Distribution then actually modifies the net amount on qualifying claim detail lines and flags the lines with a Cap Fund claim status. The claim detail lines are then processed for payment in the normal manner through keyword APUPD (Accounts Payable Update). That is, the claim detail lines are paid in the same way DIAMOND pays claims with a Payable claim status.

Prior to setting up Capitation Fund Distribution in DIAMOND, you must establish relevant agreements with the providers who are to be paid. In these agreements you stipulate certain fundamental elements necessary to the CFD process.

For details of the CFD processing and calculation logic, see Capitation/Special Fund Distribution - How DIAMOND Thinks.
