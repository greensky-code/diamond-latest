EOBs and RAs, How DIAMOND Thinks

An EOB (Explanation of Benefits) is a report that goes to a subscriber explaining what has and has not been reimbursed on behalf of a member.

An RA (Remittance Advices) is a report that goes to a provider explaining what has and has not been reimbursed.

RAs are controlled by the provider’s contract record used to process the claim. This record, PROVC, includes an indicator of whether or not this provider receives remittance advices. EOBs are controlled by the member’s eligibility record, which has a line of business code. This code ties back to a LINBS record which includes a rule for whether or not EOBs are printed under this line of business.

If the member is ineligible, the parameter record DFLTPRINTSTAT controls the RA and EOB printing. The rules for printing these reports are established separately for online professional service claims and for institutional service claims. Electronically processed claims (using the EDI functions) do not reference these rules; rather, claims for ineligible members are considered exceptions and printed out for external handling.

In the absence of entries in DFLTPRINTSTAT, neither EOBs nor RAs are printed.

Because RAs are based on a file created in keyword CKPRT, they are printed for vendors with an A/P balance equal to zero or greater dollars ($0.00+).
