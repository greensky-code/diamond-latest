Member Conditions - Overview

DIAMOND logic supports two types of member conditions:

  • Privacy - a keyword MCOND (Member Conditions) member Condition Type of Privacy represents "privacy by diagnosis" functionality

  • Pre-Existing Condition - a keyword MCOND member Condition Type of Pre-Existing, Rated-up, or Ridered-out represents "pre-existing condition" functionality

Each type of member condition enables you to trigger special processing logic for claims and authorizations that include a diagnosis code and claim effective dates that reflect a specific member condition.

Privacy by Diagnosis functionality

Member conditions privacy by diagnosis functionality enables you to do the following:

  • Define member condition rules using keyword MCOND (Member Conditions) for a Condition Type of Privacy. These rules specify when privacy applies to claims based on diagnosis codes and effective periods. They also specify when privacy applies to authorizations based on effective periods alone.

  • Use member condition rules to flag claims for privacy when they include a diagnosis code and effective period reflecting a member condition of privacy.

  • Use member condition rules to flag authorizations for possible privacy when they include an effective period reflecting a possible member condition of privacy.

  • Define a privacy address and privacy contact using keyword ALIAS (Alias/Responsible Party/Privacy).

  • Use the privacy address for claims designated as Pay to Subscriber or Pay to Dependent under certain conditions.

  • Use the claims and authorizations privacy flags in your ancillary and ad hoc reporting as appropriate.

Pre-Existing Conditions functionality

Member conditions pre-existing conditions functionality enables you to do the following:

  • Define member condition rules using keyword MCOND (Member Conditions) for a Condition Type of Pre-Existing, Rated-up, or Ridered-out. These rules specify when member pre-existing conditions logic applies to claims based on diagnosis codes and effective periods.

  • For Institutional claims, use member condition rules to pay, hold, or deny entire claims when they include a diagnosis code and effective period reflecting a member condition of pre-existing condition.

  • For Professional Services and Dental claims, use member condition rules to pay, hold, or deny individual claim detail lines when they include a diagnosis code and effective period reflecting a member condition of pre-existing condition.

Notes

  • Member conditions functionality does not impact DIAMOND automatic or manual correspondence nor the EOB/RA process. You can however, use member privacy addresses and contacts when you use third party tools for ad hoc reporting, correspondence, and EOB generation.

  • Keyword MELIG (Member Eligibility) check box Privacy On is not impacted by member conditions functionality.

  • Claims and authorizations are not automatically reevaluated when you add a member condition.

  • The Privacy Applies text boxes on keywords INCLM (Institutional Claims), PSCLM (Professional Claims), and DNCLM (Dental Claims) are not included in keyword CLDSP (Claims Display).

  • The Privacy May Apply text box on keyword AUTHS (Authorization Master) is not included in keyword AUDSP (Member Authorizations Display).

For more information on setting up member conditions functionality, see Setting Up Member Conditions.

For more information on member conditions processing logic, see Member Conditions, How DIAMOND Thinks.
