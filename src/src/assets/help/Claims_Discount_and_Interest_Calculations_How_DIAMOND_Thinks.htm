The claims discount calculation, claims penalty, and claims interest calculation functionality provides you the option to automatically calculate discount, penalty, or interest payments against payable and reversed payable claims.

Claim discounts are typically based on negotiated arrangements with providers. Some arrangements may allow for your organization to apply discounts against claim payments that are made in a timely manner (for example, within a time frame specified in a provider contract); these types of arrangements are sometimes called prompt payment discounts. Conversely, your organization may be required to pay a penalty amount, or interest, for claims that are paid late. You can set up rules at the line of business level that automatically calculate discount, penalty or interest amounts for payable claim detail lines. DIAMOND provides a manual process for you to apply discount, penalty, or interest amounts against adjusted lines.

  • Discount and interest calculations are applied only to payable (including reversed payable) claim detail lines. Discount and interest calculations are not applied against informational lines that are created during whole claim pricing, or against capitated lines or staff lines.

  • Discount and interest calculations are system calculated based on the discount and interest calculation rules your organization defines. The calculated discount and interest amounts cannot be manually entered, changed, or deleted.

  • Discount and interest amounts are calculated during the check print process against the claim’s net amount. The new amount (net amount less discount or plus interest) is stored in the Paid Net Amt field.

  • Discount and interest calculations are based on calendar days; no consideration is given to weekends or holidays.

  • When the period of time from the Calculation Date (Received Date, Reset Date, or Interest Inquiry Date) to the payment date spans two sets of discount or interest rates, DIAMOND uses the rate in effect at the payment date.

  • A single claim detail line cannot have both a discount amount and an interest amount applied. If you set your system to evaluate for both discount and interest, DIAMOND evaluates the discount calculation rules first.

  • For each line of business, you determine if you want to calculate discount, interest, both discount and interest, or neither (the default is neither). If you set a line of business to calculate both discount and interest, DIAMOND will first evaluate discount rules. If a rule is found, then DIAMOND will apply that rule and stop searching, if no discount rule is found, or if the discount calculated is 0, then DIAMOND will evaluate the interest rules to see if any are applicable.

  • The LINBS (Line of Business) screen includes a separate check box called Pay Sub/Dep. This field allows you to select a different setting for claims that are paid directly to subscribers or dependents; for example, a line of business could be set to evaluate both discount and interest calculation rules for vendor payments, while payments for the same line of business payable to the subscriber or dependent can be set so that they do not evaluate either discount or interest calculation rules.

  • Discount or interest amounts can only be manually applied to adjusted claim lines.

  • You cannot use discount and or interest calculation rules if you use DIAMOND’s partial pay functionality.

  • -You can create user-defined Reason Codes to apply to each interest, discount, or penalty assessment.

  • -You can charge interest, discounts, and penalties to separate G/L accounts. To view these accounts, use the View Additional G/L Accounts menu command available from ACPAY and APVDS.

Discount Calculations

Discounts can be applied against payable claim lines and reversed payable claim lines. Discounts are based on the number of days it takes to pay a claim. The discount amount is based on the percentage defined in the discount calculation rule; the percentage rate is multiplied against the Net Amount to determine the actual discount amount that will be subtracted from the claim payment. Discounts are calculated at the time of payment.

If a claim detail line is held for any reason prior to the line being resolved and paid, the hold reason code will not impact the discount amount applied. You cannot set a minimum amount for discount payments; therefore, a discount can be as small as $0.01.

Applying a discount to an adjusted line is a manual process. For more information, see Discounts on Adjusted Claim Lines.

Discount Evaluation is Set at the Line of Business

You use LINBS (Line of Business) to determine whether or not discount calculation rules will be evaluated for vendor payments and/or payments made directly to subscribers or dependents.

Discount Calculation Rules

You create rules in keyword LBCDC (Line of Business Claims Discount Calculations) that DIAMOND uses to determine if a discount should be applied to a claim line. For each discount calculation rule you create, the combination of claim type, rule order id, and effective date must be unique. For each discount calculation rule, you need to define the following criteria:

  • Claim Type

  • Rule Order

  • Effective Date

  • Termination Date (optional)

  • Discount Days

  • Discount Rate (percentage)

  • Search Sequence(s)

Claim Type

You must select a claim type (professional, institutional, or dental) for each rule.

Rule Order

Rule order is the sequence number assigned to the rule. This sequence number determines the order in which DIAMOND evaluates the rule. Discount calculation rules are evaluated in order from lowest number to highest number. DIAMOND stops searching as soon as it finds a rule that matches the information on the claim line; therefore, the more specific rules should have lower Rule Order numbers, while the more general rules should have higher Rule Order numbers.

Effective Date

Effective Date is required and determines the first date on which the rule may be applied to a claim.

Termination Date

Termination date is not required, but you can use it to set an end date for the discount calculation rule.

Discount Days

Discount days is the maximum number of days from the claim’s Received Date to the payment date (Check Date or EFT Date) in which the payment is considered to be timely and, therefore, eligible for a discount.

Discount Rate

Discount Rate is the percentage rate for the discount. This percentage is multiplied against the Net Amt of the claim to determine the actual discount. The discount amount is subtracted from the Net Amt to create the Paid Net Amt which is the amount that will appear on the check or the EFT payment.

Search Sequences

Each rule must have at least one search sequence and each search sequence must include either a Secondary determinant or a From value for the Primary Determinant.

Each search sequence has a number (Search Seq) that determines the order in which the search sequence is evaluated; sequences are evaluated in numerical order, from lowest number to highest.

If a rule has two or more search sequences, only claims that match the criterion of all search sequences will match the discount calculation rule.

Calculating the Discount Amount

DIAMOND first calculates the number of days it took to pay the claim by subtracting the claim’s Received Date from the date the claim line was paid (either the Check Date or EFT Date). Then DIAMOND compares the days-to-pay against the Discount Days from the discount calculation rule. DIAMOND applies the discount only if the days-to-pay is less than or equal to the Discount Days.

If DIAMOND determines that the claim is eligible for a discount, then the discount is calculated by multiplying the Discount Rate against the Net Amt of the claim line. The resulting discount amount is subtracted from the Net Amt creating the Paid Net Amt. The Paid Net Amount is the amount that will be paid on the check or EFT transaction.

Discounts on Adjusted Claim Lines

Applying a discount to an adjusted claim line is a manual process. Use the Adjust Interest/Discount Amount Special menu command available from the claim detail screen to manually enter the discount amount, and if desired, a reason code for the discount.

Interest and Penalty Calculations

Interest and penalty payments can be made on payable and reversed payable claim lines. Interest and/or penalty payments are based on the number of days it takes to pay a claim and can also consider whether or not the claim is or was ever marked as unclean. You can set a minimum amount for interest payments either by claim line or by entire claim.

An automatically calculated interest or penalty amount cannot be negative unless it is for a reversed payable line.

Applying interest to an adjusted line is a manual process. For more information, see Interest on Adjusted Claim Lines.

Penalty Applications

Penalties can be applied automatically based on rules you establish in LBCIC. In LBCIC, you can establish a flat amount penalty or a penalty that is a percentage of the net claim amount. Penalties can be also applied manually. By manually entering a negative penalty amount on the Adjust Interest/Penalty/Discount Amount Menu Command, you can apply a discount to the affected claim detail line.

Interest Evaluation is Set at the Line of Business

You use LINBS (Line of Business) to determine whether or not interest calculation rules will be evaluated for vendor payments and/or payments made directly to subscribers or dependents.

Interest Calculation Rules

You create rules in keyword LBCIC (Line of Business Claims Interest Calculation) that DIAMOND uses to determine if Interest should be applied to a claim, and if so, how many days of interest should be paid. For each interest calculation rule you create, the combination of claim type, rule order id, and effective date must be unique. For each interest calculation rule, you need to define the following criteria:

  • Claim Type

  • Rule Order

  • Effective Date

  • Termination Date (optional)

  • Interest Formula

  • Interest Rate

  • Exclude Unclean Claim Line (optional)

  • Grace Days (optional)

  • Mailing Days (optional)

  • Minimum Payable Interest per Claim (optional)

  • Minimum Payable Interest per Claim Detail Line (optional)

  • Search Sequence(s)

Claim Type

You must select a claim type (professional, institutional, or dental) for each rule.

Rule Order

Rule order is the number assigned to the rule. This number determines the order in which DIAMOND evaluates the rule. Interest calculation rules are evaluated in order from lowest number to highest number. DIAMOND stops searching as soon as it finds a rule that matches the information on the claim line; therefore, the more specific rules should have lower Rule Order numbers, while the more general rules should have higher Rule Order numbers.

Effective Date

Effective Date is the first date on which the rule may be applied to a claim.

Termination Date

Termination date is not required, but you can use it to set an end date for the interest calculation rule.

Interest Formula

You can select to calculate interest on a daily basis, a monthly basis, or a yearly basis.

Interest Rate

Interest Rate is the percentage rate for the interest calculation.

Exclude Unclean Claim Line

If your organization is tracking unclean days, you can use this checkbox to exclude any claim detail that has ever been marked as unclean from having interest applied against it. This field is not available if you are using the Conditional Interest Determination Method. For more information on unclean claims, see Unclean Claim Indicator.

Grace Days

You can set up a grace period for each interest calculation rule. The number that you specify as Grace Days is the number of days for which no interest will be applied. If the claim payment is made within the grace period, then no interest will be due on the claim line. If the claim payment is made after the grace period, interest may be applied to the claim line, but the Grace Days will be subtracted from the total number of days for which interest is paid.

Mailing Days

Mailing days applies only to claim payments made by check, not EFT payments. Mailing days are added as additional days for which to pay interest on those claims where interest is applied.

Minimum Payable Interest per Claim

You can specify the minimum dollar amount of interest to be paid against an entire claim. If the interest calculation rule is applied to a claim where the total amount of interest for the whole claim is less than the amount specified in this field, then no interest is paid.

Minimum Payable Interest per Claim Detail Line

You can specify the minimum dollar amount of interest to be paid against a single claim detail line. If the interest calculation rule is applied to a claim line where the amount of interest is less than the amount specified in this field, then no interest is paid.

Search Sequences

Each rule must have at least one search sequence and each search sequence must include either:

  • A Secondary Determinant

or

  • A From value for the Primary Determinant

Each search sequence has a number (Search Seq) that determines the order in which the search sequence is evaluated; sequences are evaluated in numerical order, from lowest number to highest.

If a rule has two or more search sequences, only claims that match the criterion of all search sequences will match the discount calculation rule.

Interest Calculation Options

DIAMOND’s interest calculation functionality is quite flexible. The following are options you can use to customize interest calculations for your organization:

  • Unclean Claim Indicator

  • Apply Unclean Indicator

  • Interest Calculation Date

  • Interest Formula

  • Interest Determination Method

Unclean Claim Indicator

Some organizations consider whether or not a claim is unclean (also known as dirty) when calculating interest payments. A claim may be considered unclean if it requires additional substantiating documentation from a source outside of your organization and if the missing information prevents you from paying the claim timely.

Unclean days are only tracked on payable and informational claim lines. If another type of claim line is changed to payable or informational, unclean days can only be tracked from the date that the line is changed.

If a claim line is deleted prior to payment, any unclean days information for the claim line is also deleted.

DIAMOND can automatically apply the unclean claim status to payable claim detail lines and to informational detail lines (lines that have undergone whole claim pricing) through the use of hold or deny reason codes. You also have the option to manually mark a claim detail line as unclean.

Mark a Claim Detail Line as Unclean

Each claim detail line includes an Unclean Indicator. The default for this indicator is no (meaning that the claim is clean). The indicator will be set to yes to indicate an unclean claim by either of the following methods:

  • Application of an unclean hold or deny reason code.

  • Use of the Reset Claim Indicator.

Unclean Hold and Deny Reason Codes

REASN (Reason Code Maintenance) includes a checkbox called Unclean Indicator. When this box is checked on a hold or deny reason code, and this reason code is applied to a claim line, that claim line’s Unclean Indicator is then checked.

Only hold or deny reason codes can change a claim’s unclean indicator to yes. If another type of reason code (besides hold or deny) has the unclean claim indicator selected, and is applied to a claim, it will not affect the claim’s Unclean Indicator.

When you check the Unclean Indicator on a reason code, it only affects claims to which it is applied after the check box is selected. Claims to which this reason code was applied, prior to the check box being selected will not be marked as unclean as a result of this reason code.

 

WARNING: Once a reason code’s Unclean Indicator has been checked, it cannot be unchecked. If you want to change the reason code so that it no longer has a checked Unclean Indicator, you have to create a new reason code.

Reset Claim Indicator

The Reset Claim Indicator is a Special menu command available from the claim detail screen that enables you to switch a claim’s Unclean Indicator from yes to no or from no to yes. Changing the Unclean Indicator from yes to no only affects the claim going forward, it does not erase any unclean days that have already accumulated.

This command is not available if the Interest Determination Method on the line of business is Conditional.

Manually Changing the Number of Unclean Days

There are two ways you can affect the number of unclean days that have accumulated on a claim detail line:

  • Override all Unclean Days

  • Deduct Some Unclean Days

These fields are available on the Claims Interest/Discount Information menu command that is available from the claim detail screens. If you want to use either of these options, you must fill in the fields prior to running APUPD and check print.

Override all Unclean Days

Selecting this field erases any unclean days that have accumulated.

Deduct Some Unclean Days

This field enables you to subtract some of the unclean days from a claim without reversing all of the unclean days.

Unclean Days on Informational Claim Lines

If an informational claim line has accumulated unclean days, those days will be transferred to the resulting system generated payable line.

Apply Unclean Indicator

The Apply Unclean Indicator is an option you set at the line of business to determine how DIAMOND applies the Unclean Indicator on claim detail lines. There are two options; you can select to have all claim detail lines set to unclean if one claim detail is unclean, or you can select to have each claim detail line evaluated separately. The default is to set all claim detail lines to unclean if one claim detail line is unclean.

Interest Calculation Date

The Interest Calculation Date is the date that is used as the From Date in interest calculations. There are three dates you can use as the Calculation Date:

  • Received Date (default)

  • Reset Date

  • Interest Inquiry Date

Received Date

The default is to use the claim’s Received Date as the calculation date.

Reset Date

Each claim detail line includes a Reset Date field. If you want to use the reset date in the interest calculation instead of the received date, you must select the Use Reset Date check box on LINBS. DIAMOND uses the Reset Date as the From Date in the interest calculations when the following criteria are met: the Reset Date check box on LINBS is checked, the Reset Date field on the claim detail line is filled in, and an interest calculation is applied. If the line of business record specifies to use the Reset Date, but the reset date field on the claim line is blank, DIAMOND uses the Received Date in the interest calculation.

Inquiry Date

If an Interest Inquiry Date is provided, it takes precedence regardless of whether or not the Use Reset date is selected on the line of business. The Interest Inquiry Date is set on the Special menu command, Claims Interest/Discount Information. This Special menu command is available from each claim detail screen.

Interest Formula

The Interest Formula field on the LBCIC screen enables you to have interest calculated at any of these intervals:

  • Daily

  • Monthly

  • Annually

Daily Interest

The Daily interest calculation formula is

For Check payments: Net Amount * Interest Rate * (Days-to-pay – Grace Days + Mailing Days)

For EFT payments: Net Amount * Interest Rate * (Days-to-pay – Grace Days)

Monthly Interest

The Monthly interest calculation formula is

For Check payments: Net Amount * Interest Rate/number of days in month * (Days-to-pay – Grace Days + Mailing Days)

For EFT payments: Net Amount * Interest Rate/number of days in month * (Days-to-pay – Grace Days)

Annual Interest

The Annual interest calculation formula is

For Check payments: Net Amount * Interest Rate/number of days in year * (Days-to-pay – Grace Days + Mailing Days)

For EFT payments: Net Amount * Interest Rate/number of days in year * (Days-to-pay – Grace Days)

Interest Determination Method

The interest determination method is set at the line of business and determines whether or not unclean days are considered in the interest calculation. There are three methods available:

  • Dynamic

  • Conditional (default)

  • Total Days

Dynamic

The dynamic method calculates the number of days against which to pay interest based on the number of days that the claim was clean. The claim may have gone back and forth between being clean and unclean. The calculation counts all days from the calculation date to the payment date, then subtracts the number of days that the claim was unclean. The number of days for which to pay interest are calculated as follows:

[Pay date (check Date or EFT Date) minus Calculation Date (Received Date, Inquiry Date, or Reset Date)] minus Unclean Days.

Dynamic Interest Determination Method Examples

Conditional

The conditional method calculates the number of days against which to pay interest based on whether or not the claim has ever been marked as unclean. If the claim has, at any point, been marked as unclean, then no interest is paid. Interest is only paid on claims that have never been marked as unclean. The number of days for which to pay interest are calculated as follows:

If the number of Unclean days is greater than 0, do not pay interest; otherwise, Pay Date (Check Date or EFT Date) minus Calculation Date (Received Date, Inquiry Date, or Reset Date)

When the conditional interest determination method is used, you cannot use the Reset Indicator to change the claims unclean setting and the Exclude Unclean Claim field on LBCIC is not available.

Conditional Interest Determination Method Examples

Total Days

The total days method calculates the number of days against which to pay interest based on the number of days it took to pay the claim, regardless of whether or not the claim has ever been marked as unclean. The number of days for which to pay interest are calculated as follows:

Pay Date (Check Date or EFT Date) minus Calculation Date (Received Date, Inquiry Date, or Reset Date)

Interest on Adjusted Claim Lines

Applying interest to an adjusted claim line is a manual process. The various options available for interest calculations, including Unclean Claim Indicator, Apply Unclean Indicator, Interest Calculation Date, Interest Formula, and Interest Determination Method are not available for adjusted claim lines. Use the Adjust Interest/Discount Amount Special menu command available from the claim detail screen to manually enter the interest amount, and if desired, a reason code for the interest.

What Happens When You Run Check Print and Check Post

Discount calculations and interest calculations are evaluated and calculated during the check print process. The following occurs for payable and reversed payable claim lines when you run check print:

  • DIAMOND evaluates discount calculation rules and/or interest calculation rules if LINBS is set to do so.

  • If DIAMOND finds a matching rule, it calculates the discount or interest amount.

  • DIAMOND counts the number of unclean days for each claim line.

The following occurs after you run check post:

  • DIAMOND updates the general ledger with any discount or interest amounts.

  • DIAMOND fills in the discount or interest information on the claim detail screen, the Claim Interest/Discount Information Special menu command, and the View Claim Interest/Discount Information Special menu command.

The General Ledger and Interest and Discount Amounts

The general ledger screen includes fields that enable you to separate claims discount monies and claims interest monies from the standard general ledger reference assignments to the primary and secondary general ledger codes.

Unclean Days on Claims in the DDS API/EDI Staging Tables

You can set up error messages that mark claim lines as unclean when applied against a claim header or claim detail in the DDS API/EDI staging tables. When these claims are moved from the staging tables into DIAMOND’s production tables, they will no longer accumulate unclean days as a result of the error message. Once in DIAMOND’s production tables, these lines will be treated as all other claim lines and can be marked as unclean by application of an unclean reason code or can be marked manually by the Reset Indicator Special menu command.

Unclean days that are accumulated while in the staging tables will be counted and will be used in interest calculations in the same way that all other unclean days are.

1099 Reporting

Interest and discount amounts are reflected in 1099 totals.

Security

The access setup in SFUNC (Function Access) for the following commands on the Special menu is separate from that for the claim detail window:

  • Claim Interest/Discount Information

  • Reset Claim Indicator

  • Adjust Interest/Discount Amounts
